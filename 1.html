Vanilin C8H8O3

Vanillin, the primary compound in vanilla, is what gives holiday treats their warm, sweet aroma. Found naturally in vanilla beans or synthesized for use in baking, this compound is key to the flavor of cookies, cakes, and festive beverages.
Vanillin can also be used in perfumes and candles, whose smell makes its way all over the house. Its fragrance and flavor is unforgettably a part of the holiday season.
